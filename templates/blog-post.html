Same company, three different stories.

Laura McAliley
Fractional CMO · 6 min read

Your website, your sales team, and the AI tools buyers now ask first probably describe your company three different ways. Here is how that happens — and what it quietly costs you.

I see it on almost every first call. The founder pitches one crisp sentence. The homepage says something adjacent. The sales deck has drifted somewhere else entirely. Each was written at a different moment, by a different person, for a different audience — and no one ever reconciled them.

Three authors, three stories

The website was written for investors. The deck was written for a specific deal that closed last year. The AI assistant is paraphrasing whatever it scraped. None of them are wrong, exactly. They just don't agree — and a buyer comparing all three reads the gaps as risk.

The product was strong. The story wasn't landing yet — because there wasn't one story to land.

The fix isn't a rewrite of any single asset. It's a source-grounded foundation every asset — and every tool — can run from. I research what's real, rate every finding with a source and a confidence level, and write it down once.

What one story changes

Sales stops improvising. The deck and the site finally say the same thing.
AI output stops sounding generic, because the input it's given is specific.
The foundation is yours to keep — with or without me.

Start with a positioning audit before you spend against it. Two weeks, one source of truth, and a clear view of where the gaps are costing you deals.
